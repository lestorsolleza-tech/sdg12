Food Conservation & Sustainability

Learn about food waste — its causes, impacts and how you can help reduce it. Discover proper storage, smart consumption, and food-label awareness to build a sustainable food culture.

Global Impact: In 2022, 1.05 billion tonnes of food waste were generated globally (≈132 kg per person), representing ~19% of food available to consumers. About 60% (631 million tonnes) occurred at the household level.
Source: UNEP Food Waste Index Report 2024, published March 27, 2024 | View Report →

What Causes Food Waste?

  • Overproduction: More food is produced than needed, often leading to surplus spoilage.
  • Poor Storage & Handling: Inadequate refrigeration, transport delays, or poor packaging shorten shelf life.
  • Consumer Behavior: Overbuying, neglecting to check inventory, or misunderstanding expiration labels.
  • Distribution & Supply Chain Losses: Food lost or wasted in transit or storage before even reaching consumers.
Local Research Finding: Studies near WVSU Janiuay Campus show that 57% of households practice meal planning and proper food storage, yet 28% still prepare more food than necessary, leading to leftovers or spoilage.

Food waste doesn't only affect what's on your plate — it impacts communities, economies, and the environment.

Environmental

Wasted food produces greenhouse gases (like methane) when decomposed, contributing to climate change. Food waste generates 8-10% of global GHG emissions — 5 times more than the aviation sector.

Source: UNEP 2024
Economic

Globally, billions worth of food are lost or wasted every year — economic loss for producers and consumers alike. The value of wasted food represents a significant drain on household budgets worldwide.

Social

Food that's wasted could instead help feed people who lack reliable access to nutritious meals. While 1.05 billion tonnes are wasted annually, hundreds of millions face food insecurity.

Adopting sustainable habits helps reduce waste and supports healthy ecosystems:

  • Plan meals and shop wisely — buy only what you need.
  • Increase plant-based meals to reduce resource strain.
  • Support local farmers / markets to cut transport emissions.
  • Avoid excessive packaging and choose reusable containers.

Food Expiration & Quality Labels

Labels like "Best Before," "Use By," or "Sell By" help consumers and retailers, but many people misunderstand what they mean.

Best Before

This indicates when the product is expected to be at peak quality (taste, texture). Food is often still safe to eat after this date.

Use By

For perishables—consider this date as the final safe-to-consume date (especially for meat, dairy, etc.).

Sell By

Used by stores to manage stock; not a safety or expiration date for consumers.

Important: Except for highly perishable items (like meat, dairy), many foods are still safe after these dates — but always check smell, texture and appearance before consuming.

Global vs Local Food Waste

In many developing countries, food waste occurs mostly during production, transport, and storage because of inadequate infrastructure — long before it reaches consumers. Meanwhile, in more urbanized or developed regions, a large portion of waste happens at the consumer or retail level due to overbuying, misunderstanding expiration labels, or improper storage.

Key Finding: UNEP 2024 data shows household food waste levels differ by only 7 kg per capita between high-income and low-income countries, suggesting the problem is universal and not solely linked to affluence.

Addressing both sides — supply chain and consumer behavior — is key to reducing global food waste.

Technology & Food Preservation

  • Smart refrigeration and temperature-controlled storage to prolong shelf life.
  • Food-tracking apps that help monitor expiry dates and consumption before spoilage.
  • Vacuum sealing, preserving, and proper packaging to reduce exposure and spoilage.
  • High-Pressure Processing (HPP) to kill pathogens without heat, preserving fresh quality.
  • Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP) to replace air with gas mixtures, inhibiting bacterial growth and oxidation.
  • Controlled Food Irradiation using radiation (e-beam/gamma) to eliminate pathogens and insects, extending shelf life.
  • Aseptic Processing and UHT Treatment to rapidly sterilize liquid food for long term, non-refrigerated storage.

Common Myths & Facts

Fact: The "Best Before" date refers to food quality, not safety. Many foods like rice, pasta, canned goods, and snacks remain safe when stored properly.

Fact: Properly stored leftovers can stay safe for 3–4 days in the refrigerator when kept in airtight containers.

Fact: Household food waste makes up 60% of total food waste globally (631 million tonnes in 2022). Small actions by many people create a huge impact.
Source: UNEP 2024

Fact: Food waste includes perfectly edible food that is thrown away due to overbuying, poor planning, or misunderstanding labels.

Fact: Many food banks and community organizations safely accept and distribute donated food to people in need.

Fact: Organic food can spoil faster due to the lack of preservatives and still contributes to waste if not stored properly.

Fact: Food waste also impacts the economy and society, wasting money, resources, and worsening food insecurity.

Fact: Composting helps reduce landfill waste, but preventing food waste before it happens is the most effective solution under SDG 12.

What You Can Do Today

  • Check your food storage and dispose only when spoilage signs appear (not just by date).
  • Plan weekly meals to avoid overbuying and reduce waste.
  • Compost or properly dispose of organic waste.
  • Share excess or near-expiry food responsibly instead of throwing away immediately.
  • Label and date leftovers to ensure you use them before they go bad.
  • Freeze excess food like herbs, meat, or cooked meals for future use.

Key Takeaways on Food Conservation

Waste at Households

Planning meals, storing food correctly, and understanding expiration labels can significantly reduce household food waste—which accounts for 60% of global total.

Choose Sustainably

Supporting local produce and reducing overconsumption helps lower emissions and strengthens local food systems.

Take Action Today

Small actions such as composting, donating surplus food, and sharing awareness contribute to global sustainability goals.
