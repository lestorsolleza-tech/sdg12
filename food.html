Food Conservation

Promoting sustainable food systems to reduce waste, protect the environment, and ensure food security for future generations.

Major Causes of Food Waste

  • Overproduction: Producing more food than needed.
  • Improper Storage: Poor refrigeration and packaging.
  • Consumer Behavior: Overbuying and poor planning.
  • Distribution Issues: Transport and infrastructure losses.

Impact of Food Waste

🌍 Environmental

Methane emissions from wasted food worsen climate change.

💰 Economic

Over $1 trillion worth of food is wasted annually.

👥 Social

Wasted food could feed millions facing hunger.

Sustainable Food Choices

  • Buy only what you need and plan meals.
  • Eat more plant-based meals.
  • Choose local and seasonal products.
  • Reduce plastic and food packaging.

Understanding Food Labels

Label Meaning
Best Before Quality may reduce, still safe to consume
Use By Food safety limit
Sell By Retail guidance only

Global vs Local Food Waste

Developing countries experience waste mainly during harvesting and storage, while developed countries waste food at consumer and retail levels.

Technology in Food Conservation

  • Smart refrigeration and temperature control
  • Expiration tracking mobile applications
  • Vacuum packaging and preservation techniques

Common Myths About Food Waste

Properly stored leftovers are safe for 3–4 days.

What You Can Do Today

  • Track and reduce food waste weekly
  • Donate surplus food responsibly
  • Compost organic waste
  • Educate family and community
